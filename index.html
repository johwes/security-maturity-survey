The Governance Gap

The Speed Trap

Your team can deploy a security patch in under an hour using automated pipelines — but your change management process requires three levels of sign-off, taking four days on average. The deployment capability is irrelevant. You remain exposed for four days. Autonomous threats operate in minutes.

The Alignment Trap

An organization that takes 12 days to approve and 13 days to deploy appears aligned on paper. In reality, it is exposed for 25 days. Matching a slow approval process with a slow deployment process does not reduce risk — it just makes both failures invisible.

The only safe outcome is absolute speed: critical vulnerabilities in internet-facing systems must go from discovery to verified deployment within 24 hours. Governance processes that cannot support this threshold must be redesigned, not optimized.

Regulatory Context

NIS2 / Cybersäkerhetslagen 2025:1506

EU Network & Information Security Directive

In force in Sweden from January 15, 2026. Covers 18 critical sectors with 50+ employees or €10M+ annual turnover.

  • Incident reporting: 24h early warning → 72h detailed notification → 1-month final report
  • Board liability: CEOs and directors personally liable for cybersecurity compliance
  • Penalties: Up to €10M or 2% of global turnover for essential entities
DORA

Digital Operational Resilience Act

Mandates demonstrable operational resilience for financial sector organizations, with direct board-level accountability.

  • CTEM alignment: Continuous Threat Exposure Management satisfies NIS2 Article 21.2(e)
  • Recovery testing: Quarterly restore drills directly support DORA resilience mandates
  • Governance velocity: Pre-approved response frameworks reduce regulatory exposure
